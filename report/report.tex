\begin{abstract}
    This report presents a collection of competitive agents in SoccerTwos, a physics-based 2v2 multi-agent soccer environment. SoccerTwos presents two challenges to training RL agents: sparse rewards and limited observations. We experimented with different training algorithms, different reward structures, and different policy models to train competent agents for the in-class competition.
\end{abstract}
